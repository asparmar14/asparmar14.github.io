Checkout Funnel Optimisation

→ Identified ~21% drop-off at Cart→Checkout and proposed 3 high-impact conversion fixes

🧠

Problem

Only ~32% of users who added to cart completed a purchase. At scale, even a 5% recovery in checkout conversion represents significant revenue — but no one had quantified where the loss was happening.

📊

Key Metrics

Insights Identified

  • ▸ ~21% drop at Cart→Checkout — largest single-stage loss in the funnel
  • ▸ Delivery fee appears only at checkout, causing price-shock abandonment
  • ▸ Mobile address entry has ~9% higher abandonment than desktop — friction in form UX
💡

Recommendations

  • ① Surface delivery fee estimate on the cart page — eliminate checkout surprise
  • ② Enable Google address autofill on mobile — reduce address entry friction
  • ③ A/B test a "Free delivery at ₹X" progress bar to lift cart value and reduce cost sensitivity
📈 Estimated Impact
Potential +8–12% checkout conversion improvement
Key Takeaways
  • → The biggest conversion leak is often a transparency issue, not a product issue
  • → Mobile UX friction creates measurably different abandonment rates than desktop
  • → Small UX changes at high-volume stages have outsized revenue impact
🥗 HelloFresh

Customer Retention Strategy

→ Diagnosed churn drivers in weeks 4–8 and designed a lifecycle-based retention framework targeting +15% improvement

🧠

Problem

Month-3 retention was ~54% — 11 points below the subscription industry benchmark of ~65%. Churn was clustering in weeks 4–8, the window before customers form a purchase habit.

📊

Key Metrics Defined

Week-1 / Week-4 / Month-3 Retention Skip Rate (churn predictor) Churn by Acquisition Channel 90-day Reactivation Rate
🔍

Insights Identified

  • ▸ Customers who skipped 2+ consecutive weeks had 3× higher churn probability
  • ▸ Early customisers (first 3 boxes) retained at 71% vs 46% for non-customisers — a 25-point gap
  • ▸ Paid social cohorts churned ~40% faster than organic — poor channel-to-intent fit
💡

Recommendations

  • ① Trigger personalised skip intervention at first skip — recipe swap prompt before cancellation path
  • ② Introduce a "build your box" onboarding flow to drive early customisation and habit formation
  • ③ Reweight paid social budget toward channels with better 90-day retention, not just lowest CAC
📈 Estimated Impact
Month-3 retention: 54% → target 67%  ·  Potential +15% retention improvement
Key Takeaways
  • → Skip rate is a leading indicator of churn — intervening early is cheaper than reactivation
  • → Early product engagement (customisation) is the strongest predictor of long-term retention
  • → Optimising for CAC alone is misleading — channel quality must be measured at 90-day LTV

02 / Geospatial Risk Analytics
Seismic Risk Pattern Analysis — India (10 Years, USGS Data)
🧠 Business Problem

Risk analysts needed a data-driven understanding of which Indian regions carry highest earthquake risk — to prioritise infrastructure investment and improve insurance risk-scoring accuracy.

🔍 Insights Identified
  • ▸Latitude is the single strongest predictor of earthquake severity — enabling simple geographic risk tiers
  • ▸Random Forest achieved ~90% classification accuracy — validated across 4 competing models
  • ▸Northeast India and Himalayan belt carry 3× higher major event frequency than peninsular India
📈 Impact
Geographically targeted risk framework enables 3× more efficient infrastructure investment vs uniform national standards
Key Takeaways
  • →Simple geographic features (latitude) outperform complex engineered variables — simpler models win
  • →Model comparison is essential — Decision Tree underperformed Random Forest by ~11 accuracy points
  • →Visual maps communicate risk to non-technical stakeholders faster than any table or report

03 / AI-Powered Analytics Tool
Document Intelligence — RAG-based QA System for Faster Insight Retrieval
🧠 Business Problem

Analytics teams spend an estimated 15–30% of their time searching documents for context — not analysing. Identified an opportunity to build a natural language query layer that surfaces answers directly from any uploaded document.

🔍 Insights & Findings
  • ▸RAG architecture delivers accurate, cited answers — eliminates hallucination risk of pure LLM approaches
  • ▸System enables stakeholder self-serve — reduces analyst interruptions for documented information
  • ▸Highest ROI in organisations with large policy or product documentation libraries
📈 Impact
Estimated 20–30% reduction in analyst time on information retrieval · Enables full stakeholder self-serve for documented insights
Key Takeaways
  • →Information retrieval is a hidden productivity drain that scales poorly with team and document growth
  • →Applicable to analytics playbooks, product specs, and internal research — not just customer-facing tools
  • →Framing the tool as a business productivity solution — not an AI experiment — is what drives adoption
